12 Things That Are Free Under Medicare
======================================

While most services come with a cost, some preventive screenings, vaccines and counseling are covered without charge

What Medicare beneficiaries must for pay out of pocket, such as monthly premiums, annual deductibles, and copayments for doctor visits and prescription drugs can be a big worry. But many enrollees may not realize is that some services won’t cost them a cent.

You can get [vaccines and screenings](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/is-wellness-care-covered-by-part-a-or-b/ "preventive services covered by medicare") for a number of illnesses and see your doctor each year for a wellness visit. You also can get counseling to help you kick smoking or manage your diabetes.

Members only

                     

You won’t be subject to any out-of-pocket charges for these services as long as you use a health care provider who participates in Medicare, or if you get your health care through a Medicare Advantage plan, an in-network medical professional.

Here’s a look at a dozen things you can get for free from Medicare. For a full list of the program’s preventive and screening services, go to [Medicare.gov](https://www.medicare.gov/coverage/preventive-screening-services "medicare preventive service coverage").

### 1\. A onetime Welcome to Medicare checkup in your first year

This visit must be made within the first 12 months of signing up for Medicare Part B, the part of Medicare that covers doctor visits and other outpatient services.

Think of this initial visit as a [baseline check-in](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/welcome-to-medicare-visit/ "welcome to medicare visit"). Your provider will review your medical and personal history; see what medications you are taking; give you a flu and [pneumococcal shot](https://www.aarp.org/health/drugs-supplements/info-2020/pneumonia-vaccine.html "pneumonia vaccine"); monitor your vital signs including blood pressure, height and weight; do a simple vision test; ask some mental and behavioral health questions; and offer to help you create any [advance directives](https://www.aarp.org/caregiving/financial-legal/free-printable-advance-directives/ "advance directives") you might not yet have.

All this will be free.

But after this general check-in if your doctor wants to order some diagnostic tests or perform some other services, the rules governing Part B will apply and you’ll be [responsible for 20 percent](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-are-costs-for-part-b/ "medicare part b copayments") of the costs. Your coinsurance or copay may be different if you are enrolled in a [Medicare Advantage plan](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2020/original-medicare-vs-advantage.html "medicare advantage vs original medicare") or if you have supplemental, or Medigap, insurance.

### 2. A wellness visit every year after that helps keep you healthy

The first thing to know about this is what it isn’t: a physical. This is a [once-a-year visit](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-the-annual-wellness-visit/ "medicare yearly wellness visit") to “update your personalized plan to help prevent disease or disability,” according to Medicare.

Your doctor will probably take your vital signs and review your medical history and medications. Providers are also expected to do a cognitive assessment to look for any [signs of dementia](https://www.aarp.org/caregiving/health/info-2019/dementia-warning-signs.html "dementia warning signs").

If during this visit your doctor needs to order some tests, such as blood work or an actual physical exam, then as with that first welcome Medicare visit, the rules governing your coinsurance under Medicare Part B will apply.

### 3. Vaccines are meant to prevent many severe illnesses

Thanks to a federal law passed in 2022, Medicare now [covers most vaccines](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-vaccines-does-medicare-cover/ "vaccines covered by medicare") at no cost to enrollees who have Medicare Part D prescription plans or Medicare Advantage drug coverage.

This includes inoculations such as the [shots for shingles](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-shingles-shot/ "shingles vaccine and medicare") and [RSV](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2023/who-needs-rsv-vaccine.html "who needs rsv vaccine") that the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) recommends. Medicare Part B already had covered other vaccines, such as [seasonal flu](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/do-orig-medicare-and-part-c-cover-flu-shot/ "does medicare cover flu shots") and [COVID-19 shots](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2023/are-covid-vaccines-still-free.html "are covid vaccines still free"), at no charge.

### 4\. Alcohol counseling points out why age, alcohol don't mix

Medicare will cover one alcohol misuse screening each year.

In addition, the program will cover up to four brief face-to-face counseling sessions each year if you are someone [who uses alcohol](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2022/age-and-alcohol.html "age and alcohol") but doesn’t meet the medical criteria for being alcohol dependent. You must get the counseling in a [primary care setting](https://www.aarp.org/benefits-discounts/members-only-access/info-2024/sharon-malone-grown-women-talk-excerpt.html "choosing a primary care physician"), such as a doctor’s office or clinic.

**Quiz:** [Alcohol and your health](https://www.aarp.org/health/healthy-living/info-2019/alcohol-quiz.html "alcohol health quiz")

### 5\. Colorectal screenings catch possible cancer early

Four separate screening tests [for colorectal cancer](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2021/colorectal-cancer.html "warning signs of colorectal cancer") are covered for free:

*   A [fecal occult blood test](https://www.medicare.gov/coverage/fecal-occult-blood-tests "medicare fecal blood occult test") every 12 months, starting at age 45. This will detect blood in the stool.

*   A [flexible sigmoidoscopy](https://www.medicare.gov/coverage/flexible-sigmoidoscopies "flexible sigmoidoscopy") every 48 months, also starting at age 45, with the proviso that you haven’t had a colonoscopy in the past 10 years.

*   A [stool DNA test](https://www.medicare.gov/coverage/multi-target-stool-dna-tests "medicare and stool DNA tests") every three years for people 45 to 85 who are not at high risk for colon cancer and don’t have symptoms of colorectal disease.

*   A screening [colonoscopy every 10 years](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-a-colonoscopy/ "does medicare cover a colonoscopy") if you are not at high risk for colon cancer, or every two years if you have a history of colon problems or a family history of colon cancer.

### 6\. Depression screening can help combat isolation  

Medicare covers [one depression screening a year](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-mental-health/ "does medicare cover mental health") that must happen in a primary care setting, like a doctor’s office. Suicide rates among older men increase with age.

If a provider recommends follow-up treatment or other mental health and behavioral services, then the coinsurance rules under Part B would apply. [In a crisis](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2021/suicide-risk-signs.html "warning signs of suicide"), people can contact the free and confidential [Suicide & Crisis Lifeline](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2024/988-suicide-hotline-number.html "suicide prevention hotline") by calling or texting 988 at any time.

### 7\. Diabetes screenings can stop prediabetes from progressing

Medicare Part B will cover up to two [diabetes screenings](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-diabetes/ "medicare diabetes coverage") each year, including blood glucose tests, if your doctor determines you are at [risk for developing diabetes](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/medicare-coverage-for-pre-diabetes-mdpp/ "medicare and prediabetes") and you have risk factors such as high blood pressure, a history of [abnormal cholesterol levels](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2022/ways-to-lower-cholesterol.html "how to lower cholesterol"), are obese or have a history of high blood sugar.

Part B will also cover these screenings if two or more of the following conditions apply:

*   You are 65 or older.
*   You’re overweight.
*   You have a family history of diabetes.
*   Or you’ve had diabetes while you were pregnant, often called gestational diabetes. 

**Quiz:** [How much do you know about prediabetes?](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-11-2012/prediabetes-quiz.html "prediabetes quiz")

### 8\. Lung cancer screenings are meant for smokers, ex-smokers

Medicare will cover a [lung cancer screening](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2023/lung-cancer-screening-guidelines.html "lung cancer screening guidelines") using low-dose computed tomography, a special kind of X-ray, once a year if all these conditions apply:

*   You’re age 50 to 77.
*   You have no signs or [symptoms of lung cancer](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2023/warning-signs-of-lung-cancer.html "lung cancer warning signs").
*   You either smoke now or quit smoking in the past 15 years.
*   You have a history of smoking one pack a day for a 20-year period.
*   You get an order from your doctor or other health care provider.

### 9. Mammograms can discover breast cancer in early stages

Medicare will pay for [a screening mammogram](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-mammograms/ "does medicare cover mammograms") every 12 months if you are a woman [age 40 or older](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2023/mammogram-guidelines-breast-cancer-screening.html "start mammograms at age 40"). About a quarter of women ages 50 to 74 are [not up to date](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2024/barriers-to-breast-cancer-screenings.html) on routine mammograms, the CDC says.

Be advised that if you get a diagnostic mammogram, which a doctor typically will order after a screening mammogram shows some suspicious results and they want to take a closer look, then the rules governing Part B will apply and you will be responsible for 20 percent of the cost.

That would also apply if a doctor orders [a breast ultrasound](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2023/womens-health-medical-breakthroughs.html "ultrasound for better mammograms") because the Medicare enrollee has [dense breasts](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2023/dense-breast-tissue.html "dense breast tissue"). Medicare will cover diagnostic mammograms more often than once a year if a doctor says they are medically necessary.

### 10\. Nutrition therapy helps you thrive with chronic conditions

Medicare will cover some [nutrition services](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-nutrition-counseling/ "does medicare cover nutrition counseling") if you have diabetes or kidney disease or received a kidney transplant in the past 36 months. These services can include an initial nutrition and lifestyle assessment, individual or group sessions, help managing the lifestyle factors that affect your diabetes, and follow-up visits to check on your progress.

**Quiz:** [How healthy are your cooking and eating habits?](https://www.aarp.org/health/healthy-living/info-2023/cooking-and-eating-quiz.html "cooking habits quiz")

### 11. Prostate cancer tests allow comparisons through the years

Staring the day after a man turns 50, Medicare will cover a [prostate specific antigen](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-prostate-cancer-screening/ "medicare prostate screening coverage") (PSA) blood test every 12 months. If you also need a digital rectal exam or other follow-up services, then the 20 percent coinsurance rules for Medicare Part B will apply.

### 12. Smoking counselin**g’s** **goal is to get you to quit**

Medicare will pay for up to eight counseling sessions over a 12-month period to [help you stop smoking](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-tobacco-prevention/ "smoking cessation medicare coverage") or using tobacco. Not only is smoking the cause of most lung cancers, is linked to [high blood pressure](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2024/smoking-and-high-blood-pressure.html "smoking and high blood pressure"), which is a risk [for heart attacks](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2024/heart-attack-vs-cardiac-arrest.html "heart attack vs. cardiac arrest") and [strokes](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2024/stroke-what-to-do.html "what to do during a stroke").

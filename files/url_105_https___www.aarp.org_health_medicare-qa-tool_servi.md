What services are not covered under Medicare Part B? ﻿

| Many services you’ve come to rely on when you received insurance through your employer won’t be paid for using [original Medicare](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-original-medicare/ "what is original medicare"), the two-part program that then-President Lyndon B. Johnson signed into law in 1965.

[**Part A**](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-medicare-part-a/ "what is medicare part a") provides coverage for [inpatient stays in hospitals](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-hospital-costs-are-covered-under-part-a/ "services covered by medicare part a") and skilled nursing facilities, [some home care](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-home-healthcare/ "does medicare cover home health care") and end-of-life [hospice care](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-hospice-care/ "does medicare cover hospice care").

Members only

                     

[**Part B**](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-medicare-part-b/ "what is medicare part b") covers doctors’ services, diagnostic screenings, lab tests, outpatient care, and [some medical equipment](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-medical-supplies/ "does medicare cover medical supplies") and transportation. It also covers a variety of [preventive measures](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/is-wellness-care-covered-by-part-a-or-b/ "does medicare cover preventative services") — screenings, tests and vaccines — at no cost to you.

### **What are the details of what Part B won’t pay for?**

Part B [doesn’t cover everything](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2018/services-not-covered.html "services not covered "), including several doctor and outpatient services.

*   **Care not considered medically necessary,** such as elective cosmetic surgery or [weight loss procedures ,](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-weight-loss-programs-surgery/ "does medicare cover weight loss procedures") unless you meet certain conditions associated with morbid obesity

*   [**Chiropractor**](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-chiropractic-care/ "does medicare cover chiropractic care") **or** [**acupuncture**](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-acupuncture/ "does medicare cover acupuncture") **visits﻿,** unless you meet very specific criteria. It won’t cover regular maintenance or preventive chiropractic visits or treatments to other parts of the body.

Part B covers only manual manipulation of the spine considered medically necessary to correct a subluxation, a dislocation or misalignment of the vertebrae. It covers acupuncture only if you’ve experienced low back pain for at least 12 weeks.

*   [**Dental cleanings, exams**](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-dental-care/ "does medicare cover dental care") **and X-rays,** as well as most dental care and procedures

*   [**Gym memberships**](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-gym-memberships/ "does medicare cover gym memberships") and general fitness benefits

*   **Medical services outside the United States,** except under [rare circumstances](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-me-outside-the-us/ "does medicare cover you abroad")

*   **Most prescription drugs.** Part D covers many of the doctor-prescribed medicines you take yourself as well as [insulin you inject yourself](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-insulin/ "does medicare cover insulin") and related supplies. Part B does cover drugs administered in a doctor’s office or outpatient clinic, such as most infused and injectable medications, and those delivered through durable medical equipment, including insulin from an external infusion pump that you reuse.

*   [**Routine eye exams**](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-vision-care/ "does medicare cover vision care")**, ﻿[e y eglasses and contact lenses](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-glasses/ "does medicare cover glasses")**. Part B covers eye exams to check for certain conditions, such as [glaucoma](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2019/glaucoma-eye-disease.html "glaucoma") and [diabetic retinopathy](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2019/diabetic-retinopathy.html "diabetic retinopathy") if you’re considered at high risk. It also covers one set of glasses or contact lenses if you have [cataract surgery](https://www.aarp.org/health/conditions-treatments/info-2021/cataract-surgery.html "cataract surgery") that implants an intraocular lens.

*   [**Routine foot care**](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-foot-care/ "does medicare cover foot care")**,** but it covers﻿ therapeutic shoes and accompanying orthotic inserts and some foot exams for people with diabetes

Others, including [flu](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/do-orig-medicare-and-part-c-cover-flu-shot/ "does medicare cover flu shots"), hepatitis B and pneumonia shots, are a Part B free preventive service.

### **Do Medicare Advantage or Medigap cover these services?**

[](https://www.aarp.org/membership/benefits/all-offers-a-z/)

Members only

                     

Yes, in some cases. Most [Medicare Advantage plans](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-medicare-advantage/ "what is medicare advantage") provide some dental, hearing and vision coverage. Many also cover gym memberships and fitness programs.

Medicare Advantage plans are a private [alternative to original Medicare](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2020/original-medicare-vs-advantage.html "medicare vs advantage"). By law, they must cover the same services as Part A and Part B but often have different out-of-pocket costs and usually include extra benefits.

To find a Medicare Advantage plan with these benefits, go to the [Medicare Plan Finder](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2022/advantage-enrollment.html "medicare plan finder") and type in your zip code and check **Medicare Advantage Plan.** You can type in your medications or go directly to a list of Medicare Advantage plans in your area. In the **Plan Benefits** box, you’ll see green check marks noting plans that offer vision, dental, hearing or fitness benefits. For more information about these benefits, click **Plan Details** and scroll down to **Extra Benefits.** You can visit the plan’s website or contact the plan directly for more information.

Unlike original Medicare, [Medicare Advantage plans](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/finding-the-best-plan-for-me/ "finding the best medicare plan") generally have provider networks, and they may charge more or may not cover doctors, facilities or other providers outside the plan’s network. You may also need a referral from your primary care doctor to see a specialist. Contact the plan to find out if the providers you want are covered﻿, to avoid being denied.

If you have original Medicare, you may get coverage for a package of extra benefits by buying a [Medicare supplemental plan](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2017/choosing-right-medigap-plan.html "finding the right medigap plan"), also called Medigap. These plans help pay Medicare’s deductibles and copayments, and some plans also offer dental discounts, hearing and vision care, and gym membership.

#### **Keep in mind**

If Medicare denies a claim that you believe should have been covered as a medically necessary service, you have [several levels of appeals](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/how-to-appeal-medicare-claims/ "how to appeal medicare plans"). Check your [Medicare Summary Notice](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/medicare-summary-notice/ "what is a medicare summary notice"), the claims statement you receive from Medicare, for more information about whether it paid your claims and the steps for appealing a denial.

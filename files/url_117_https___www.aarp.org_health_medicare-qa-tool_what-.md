What is Medicare Part B?
========================

* * *

By

Kimberly Lankford,

AARP

Comments

[En español](https://www.aarp.org/espanol/salud/medicare-preguntas-respuestas/que-es-medicare-parte-b/)

Published July 22, 2022

/ Updated May 09, 2024

*   [Facebook](http://www.facebook.com/sharer/sharer.php?u=https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-medicare-part-b.html)
*   [Twitter](http://twitter.com/intent/tweet?url=https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-medicare-part-b.html&related=CreateTheGood&text=What%20is%20Medicare%20Part%20B?)
*   [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/shareArticle?mini=true&url=https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-medicare-part-b.html&title=What%20is%20Medicare%20Part%20B?)

One of [Medicare’s four main parts,](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-01-2011/understanding_medicare_the_plans.html "understanding medicare's plans") Part B covers doctors’ services, diagnostic screenings, lab tests, preventive services﻿ and outpatient care, plus some [medical equipment](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-medical-supplies/ "does medicare cover medical equipment") and transportation.

The other types of coverage fall under:

[**Part A**](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-medicare-part-a/ "what is medicare part a")**,** which helps pay for [inpatient stays in hospitals](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-hospital-costs-are-covered-under-part-a/ "hospital services covered by part a") and skilled nursing facilities, some home care and end-of-life [hospice care](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-hospice-care/ "does medicare cover hospice"). Together, Part A and Part B are known as [original Medicare](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-original-medicare/ "what is original medicare").

**Part C,** also known as [Medicare Advantage](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-medicare-advantage/ "what is medicare advantage"), which is a private health insurance alternative to federally run original Medicare. You still need to sign up for Medicare Parts A and B if you [choose to get coverage](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2020/original-medicare-vs-advantage.html "medicare vs medicare advantage") from a Medicare Advantage plan.

**Part D,** which is [prescription drug coverage](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2018/part-d-prescription-drugs.html "medicare part d for prescription drugs"). You can get Part D through a stand-alone policy or from a Medicare Advantage plan.

Members only

                     

### **What services does Medicare Part B cover?**

Part B covers the bulk of your medical needs beyond hospitalization and prescription drugs, including:

*   Doctor visits or appointments with other health care providers, including some doctor services when hospitalized.
*   [Diabetes care](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-diabetes/ "medicare diabetes coverage"), including education, certain equipment, prevention programs and screenings.
*   Diagnostic tests, including CT scans, electrocardiograms, MRIs and X-rays.
*   Durable medical equipment, such as wheelchairs and walkers, that your doctor prescribes for use in your home.
*   [Emergency department](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-emergency-room-visits/ "medicare emergency room coverage") and outpatient surgery center services, as well as other outpatient hospital services.
*   Some health programs, such as cardiac rehabilitation, [obesity counselin g](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-weight-loss-programs-surgery/ "medicare weight loss coverage") and [smoking cessation.](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-tobacco-prevention/ "medicare smoking cessation coverage")
*   Laboratory services, such as blood and urine tests.
*   A limited number of prescription drugs that you usually don’t administer yourself, such as [insulin](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-insulin/ "does medicare cover insulin") from an external insulin pump that’s not disposable. Insulin you administer yourself is covered under Medicare Part D instead.
*   Outpatient ﻿[mental health service s.](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-mental-health/ "does medicare cover mental health ")
*   Outpatient [physical therapy](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-physical-therapy/ "does medicare cover physical therapy"), occupational therapy and speech and language pathology services.
*   [Preventive care](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/is-wellness-care-covered-by-part-a-or-b/ "medicare preventive care coverage"), such as [flu shots](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/do-orig-medicare-and-part-c-cover-flu-shot/ "does medicare cover flu shots") and [mammograms](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-mammograms/ "does medicare cover mammograms"), to help avert illness or detect it at an early stage. Many preventive services are covered without deductibles or copayments; however, some vaccinations, such as the [shingles vaccine](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-shingles-shot/ "does medicare cover shingles vaccines"), are covered under Part D instead.
*   A [Welcome to Medicare checkup](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/welcome-to-medicare-visit/ "welcome to medicare visit") and the [annual wellness visit](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-the-annual-wellness-visit/ "medicare wellness visit"), which are covered in full without deductibles or copayments, unless additional tests are ordered. ﻿

Even though Part B covers many doctor services, it [doesn’t cover everything](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/services-not-covered-by-medicare-part-b/ "not covered by medicare part b"). Part B generally doesn’t cover routine [dental](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-dental-care/ "does medicare cover dental "), [hearing](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-hearing-tests/ "does medicare cover hearing") and [vision care](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-vision-care/ "does medicare cover vision").

What’s more, care not considered medically necessary, such as elective cosmetic surgery, isn’t covered, either. [Chiropractor](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-chiropractic-care/ "medicare chiropractic coverage") and [acupuncture](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-acupuncture/ "does medicare cover acupuncture") visits are paid for only if you meet very specific criteria, and Part B doesn’t cover medical services outside the United States, except in [rare circumstances](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-me-outside-the-us/ "does medicare cover you abroad").

### How much does Medicare Part B cost?

Even though Medicare Part B covers many of your expenses for doctor services and outpatient care, you will have some out-of-pocket costs.

**Part B premiums.** Most people pay $174.70 a month in 2024 for [Medicare Part B premiums.](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-are-costs-for-part-b/ "costs for medicare part b") People who are single with an adjusted gross income of more than $103,000 or married filing jointly with income greater than $206,000 pay a high-income surcharge (officially called the Income﻿-Related Monthly Adjustment Amount, or IRMAA) with premiums ranging from $244.60 to $594.00 a month, [depending on income level](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2022/how-to-lower-medicare-premiums.html "how income affects medicare premiums").

Premiums are based on your last tax return on file, which is usually 2022 income for 2024 premiums.

If you don’t enroll in Medicare [when you’re first eligible](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2020/enrolling-in-medicare.html "enrolling in medicare") or qualify for a [special enrollment period](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2022/special-enrollment-period.html "medicare special enrollment period") to sign up later, you may have to pay a [late enrollment penalty](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/how-much-is-the-part-b-late-enrollment-penalty/ "medicare late enrollment penalty"), which is added to your Part B premiums for as long as you have the coverage.

**Deductible.** You must pay a $240 deductible in 2024 before most Part B coverage begins. However, the Welcome to Medicare checkup, the annual wellness visit and some preventive care are not subject to any deductible or coinsurance unless your doctor orders additional tests.

**Coinsurance.** After paying the deductible, you’ll generally pay 20 percent of the Medicare-approved amount for most doctor and outpatient services and for durable medical equipment.

**Extra charges.** Doctors who don’t [accept assignment](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/define-term-medicare-assignment/ "what is medicare assignment"), which means they don't agree to take the amount Medicare approves for their services, can charge up to 15 percent above the Medicare-approved amount for a service, which you would have to pay in addition to the 20 percent copayment for doctor services.

#### Keep in mind

**Want Medicare Advantage?** The Medicare Advantage plan must provide as least as much coverage as original Medicare, but you may have different out-of-pocket costs. For example, rather than the Part B deductible and 20 percent coinsurance, a Medicare Advantage plan may have no copayments for in-network primary doctor visits, a $20 copay for diagnostic tests and procedures, $40 copay for specialists and $90 copay for emergency care.

You may be required to use in-network doctors and other providers to receive coverage from the Medicare Advantage plan or have to pay more if you use out-of-network services, depending on the plan.

**Need [financial help](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2022/financial-assistance.html "financial assistance medicare") with Medicare’s costs?** If you have low income and assets, you may qualify for a [Medicare Savings Program](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2022/medicare-savings-program.html "medicare savings program") that helps pay your Medicare premiums and out-of-pocket costs.

Let’s face it: Medicare decisions can be complicated.

If you [miss key deadlines](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-are-the-medicare-enrollment-periods/ "medicare enrollment periods") or make mistakes, you could end up with gaps in coverage, [lifetime penalties](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/how-much-is-the-part-b-late-enrollment-penalty/ "late enrollment penalties") or extra expenses. If you need help [enrolling in Medicare](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2020/enrolling-in-medicare.html "enrolling in medicare") or making [decisions about your coverage](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2020/original-medicare-vs-advantage.html "medicare vs medicare advantage"), there are several national and state resources that can provide assistance.

### State Health Insurance Assistance Programs

State Health Insurance Assistance Programs, known as SHIP, are available in every state and the District of Columbia, Guam, Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands.

About 12,500 SHIP counselors provide free one-on-one advice, seminars and outreach programs. SHIP counselors are available in person or online to answer questions about initial Medicare enrollment or to help review your options when choosing a [Medicare Advantage](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-medicare-advantage/ "what is medicare advantage") or [Part D prescription drug plan](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2018/part-d-prescription-drugs.html "medicare part d ") during Medicare’s [annual open enrollment period](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2022/open-enrollment.html "open enrollment") from Oct. 15 to Dec. 7.

States use different names for these programs, such as the Health Insurance Counseling and Advocacy Program (HICAP) in California and Serving Health Insurance Needs of Elders (SHINE) in Florida. To find a SHIP in your area, visit [SHIPhelp.org](https://www.shiphelp.org/ "State Health Insurance Assistance Program") or call 877-839-2675.

### Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services

Working under the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) runs the Medicare program. Visit [medicare.gov](https://www.medicare.gov/ "medicare.gov") for information about how Medicare works, what it covers, when to enroll, [how to get started with Medicare](https://www.medicare.gov/basics/get-started-with-medicare "how to get started with medicare"), and how to buy private insurance policies that can fill in coverage gaps.

Its [Medicare Plan Finder](https://www.medicare.gov/plan-compare/#/?year=2024&lang=en "medicare plan finder") is a great way to learn about Part D, Medicare Advantage and Medigap plans available in your area. It also helps you compare costs for your medications and health care needs.

### Social Security Administration

While CMS runs the Medicare program, the Social Security Administration handles [Medicare enrollment](https://www.ssa.gov/medicare "social security and medicare enrollment"), even if you haven’t yet claimed Social Security retirement benefits. The SSA can be a helpful source of information about signing up for Medicare, applying for the Part D prescription drug [Extra Help program](https://www.ssa.gov/medicare/part-d-extra-help "medicare extra help"), and understanding Medicare’s [higher-income premium surcharges](https://www.ssa.gov/benefits/medicare/medicare-premiums.html "income and medicare premiums").

Call its national toll-free phone number at 800-772-1213﻿ or look up the address and phone number of your [local Social Security office](https://www.ssa.gov/locator/ "local social security office locator").​

![Image 305: spinner image](https://cdn.aarp.net/etc/uxdia/images/uxdia-spinner.svg)    ![Image 306: screenshot of social security local office lookup with the locate an office by zip code button circled](https://cdn.aarp.net/content/dam/aarpe/en/home/health/medicare-insurance/info-2022/help-enrolling/_jcr_content/root/container_main/container_body_main/container_body3/container_body_cf/body_three_cf_one/par6/articlecontentfragme/cfimage.coreimg.50.932.png/content/dam/aarp/health/medicare_insurance/2023/04/1140x655-ssa-local-office-lookup.png) 

### Medicare Rights Center

This nonprofit has been helping people navigate Medicare for more than 30 years.

Counselors are on hand to answer questions about Medicare rules, deadlines, enrollment, choices and claims. You can reach them through the organization’s national help line at 800-333-4114.

Visit its website, [medicareinteractive.org](https://www.medicareinteractive.org/ "Medicare Rights Center"), for answers to Medicare questions and more information about what’s new in Medicare.

### State insurance departments

Individual states’ insurance departments are also helpful in providing information about supplemental Medigap policies in your area. Most states list each company’s Medigap premiums for the year. Visit the [National Association of Insurance Commissioners](https://content.naic.org/state-insurance-departments "National Association of Insurance Commissioners"), look for your state in the **State Department** drop-down menu, and you’ll find a link to the state’s website.

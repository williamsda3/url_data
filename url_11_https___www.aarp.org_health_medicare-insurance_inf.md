How to Choose and Enroll in a Medicare Advantage Plan

Take these steps to compare your options with the Medicare Plan Finder

After you [enroll in Medicare](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/turning-65-when-to-apply-for-medicare/ "when to apply for medicare"), your first order of business is to decide whether you want to get coverage from [original Medicare](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-original-medicare/ "what is original medicare") — the government-run program that includes [Part A hospitalization coverage](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-medicare-part-a/ "what is medicare part a") and [Part B doctor and outpatient services](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-medicare-part-b/ "what is medicare part b") — or a [Medicare Advantage plan](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-medicare-advantage/ "what is medicare advantage"), which bundles Parts A and B and usually [Part D prescription drug coverage](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2018/part-d-prescription-drugs.html "medicare part d for prescription drugs"). Private insurers sell Medicare Advantage plans, also known as Part C.

**Most Medicare Advantage plans pay for services** that original Medicare doesn’t cover, such as [dental](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-dental-care/ "does medicare cover dental care"), [hearing](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-hearing-tests/ "does medicare cover hearing tests"), [vision care](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/does-medicare-cover-vision-care/ "does medicare cover vision care") and prescription drugs. But they typically have provider networks, and you have to pay more or may not have coverage for a provider who is out of the plan’s network. You can enroll in a Medicare Advantage plan during the seven-month [initial enrollment period](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2022/initial-enrollment-period.html "medicare initial enrollment period") surrounding your 65th birthday after you’ve enrolled in Parts A and B.

Members only

                     

You can also sign up or switch plans during the annual [open enrollment](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2022/open-enrollment.html "medicare open enrollment") period, which runs from Oct. 15 to Dec. 7 each year. Your coverage will start Jan. 1. Because Medicare Advantage plan premiums, medical and drug coverage, out-of-pocket costs and provider networks can change from year to year, it’s a good idea to review your options during open enrollment each year. You may be able to sign up at other times if you qualify for a [special enrollment period](https://www.medicare.gov/basics/get-started-with-medicare/get-more-coverage/joining-a-plan/special-enrollment-periods "medicare special enrollment periods"). And if you already have a Medicare Advantage plan, you can switch plans or go back to original Medicare during the Medicare Advantage open enrollment period from Jan. 1 to March 31.

Keep in mind that if you decide to return to original Medicare and you have a [preexisting condition](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2022/buying-medigap-policy.html "buying a medigap policy"), you may have a difficult time getting a Medigap policy.

### ﻿**8 steps to choose and sign up for Medicare Advantage**

The best way to [compare Medicare Advantage plans](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/finding-the-best-plan-for-me/ "choosing best medicare advantage plan") available in your area is by using [Medicare’s Plan Finder](https://www.medicare.gov/plan-compare "medicare plan finder") tool. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

**1.** You’ll see two options: Log in to your [online Medicare account](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/medicare-online-account/ "online medicare accounts"), or go to the second section, where you’ll enter your ZIP code and may need to select your county.

Choose **Medicare Advantage Plan** from the **Select a plan type** drop-down menu. Click the blue **Apply** button and then the green **Start** button.

![Image 297: spinner image](https://cdn.aarp.net/etc/uxdia/images/uxdia-spinner.svg)    ![Image 298: explore your options](https://aarp.widen.net/content/j0xsd36f8e/png/1140-medicare-advantage-enrollment-screenshot-1.png?crop=true&anchor=0,0&color=ffffffff&u=k2e9ec&w=1140&h=655)

**3.** If you don’t receive help, you’ll be asked if you want to see your drug costs when you compare plans. Click **Yes,** so you can get the best sense of how much you might spend with each plan.

For example, select **Plan Benefits** and check **Vision**, **Dental**, and **Hearing**. Under **Drug Coverage,** you can choose **Includes drug coverage.** And [**Special Needs Plans**](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-is-a-medicare-advantage-special-needs-plan/ "medicare special needs plans") let you find a plan that provides coverage for a [chronic condition](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2021/medicare-advantage-and-chronic-conditions.html "chronic condition benefits"), such as diabetes or end-stage renal disease.

You can sort your list. The default is **Lowest drug + premium cost,** but you can change that to **Lowest yearly drug deductible,** **Lowest health plan deductible** or **Lowest monthly premium.**

If a plan looks enticing, be sure to investigate the types of procedures and items covered and their costs before committing.

Click the **Plan Details** button for more information about coverage and out-of-pocket expenses, like copayments for doctor appointments, diagnostic tests, X-rays, emergency care, inpatient and outpatient hospital coverage, physical and occupational therapy, and mental health services.

To see if your doctors are in network, visit the plan’s website. When you click **Plan Details,** you’ll see a **Plan website** link at the top of the listing.​ A link to **View Provider Network Directory** is under doctor services, which can also direct you to a plan’s website.

**8.** To sign up for a Medicare Advantage plan, click **Enroll**. You’ll need your Medicare number and the date your parts A and B coverage started.

Other methods:

*   You may be able to enroll by calling 800-MEDICARE (800-633-4227).
*   To sign up directly with the private insurer, click **Plan Details** and look for the plan’s phone number and website.
*   You can request an application and mail it.
*   An insurance agent may be able to help you enroll.

For additional help signing up for a Medicare Advantage plan, contact your [State Health Insurance Assistance Program](https://www.shiphelp.org/ "State Health Insurance Assistance Program").

After you’ve signed up for a plan, you may be required to pay a [monthly premium to the insurance company](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2023/medicare-advantage-premiums.html "medicare advantage premiums"), although many plans don’t charge one. You’ll also pay your [Part B premiums](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-qa-tool/what-are-costs-for-part-b/ "costs for medicare part b") to Medicare. Find out if you are eligible for [financial assistance](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/info-2022/financial-assistance.html "medicare financial assistance") to help pay for Medicare premiums and out-of-pocket costs
